A week ago, I thought AI was mainly a chatbot.
This week, I built reusable Skills, configured specialized AI subagents, connected an MCP server, and used Claude Code to scaffold an entire Terraform project.
That completely changed how I think about AI and DevOps.
During Week 2 of the DevOps Micro Internship, I didn't just learn new concepts—I applied them through real project-based assignments.
One of the biggest mindset shifts for me was understanding that Agentic AI isn't just about answering prompts. It follows structured workflows, uses tools, verifies its work, and helps automate engineering tasks while still requiring human guidance.
Some of my biggest takeaways were:
✅ Understanding why CLAUDE.md guides AI differently from README.md.
✅ Creating reusable Skills and seeing /scaffold-terraform generate a complete Terraform project.
✅ Learning how Subagents, MCP, Hooks, Permissions, and Memory work together to make AI-assisted DevOps more structured, secure, and reliable.
The biggest lesson, however, wasn't technical.
I discovered that I don't learn by memorizing commands.
I learn by asking "Why?"
Whenever I understand how a tool fits into the overall DevOps workflow, the concepts become much easier to understand and remember.
I also learned not to panic when things go wrong.
While configuring the GitHub MCP server, I initially focused on the error instead of the cause. By slowing down, reading the output carefully, and troubleshooting step by step, I eventually resolved the issue. That experience reminded me that error messages are part of engineering—they're clues that help us solve problems.
Week 2 reinforced something I'll carry throughout my DevOps journey:
DevOps isn't about memorizing commands. It's about understanding systems, building practical solutions, and using automation responsibly.
Agentic AI can automate many tasks, but engineers are still responsible for providing direction, reviewing outputs, and making sound engineering decisions.
